About

I am a PhD candidate at the Institute of Automation, Chinese Academy of Sciences, working on machine learning methods for graph combinatorial optimization. My current research focuses on graph neural networks, reinforcement learning, continuous relaxations, optimal transport, stochastic control, and local search for NP-hard graph optimization problems such as Maximum Independent Set, Maximum Cut, and Maximum Clique.

I am interested in building learning-based optimizers that combine principled optimization formulations with scalable neural inference and robust empirical evaluation.

Publications

Optimal Transport–Guided Stochastic Control for Graph Combinatorial Optimization

Yang Huang, Yifan Zhang, Jian Cheng

International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2026 · Regular

We formulate graph combinatorial optimization problems as QUBO objectives, keep the exact multilinear relaxation over the continuous domain, and derive an OT-guided stochastic control framework for sampling low-energy solutions with GNN-amortized control.

Code and paper

Learning to Destroy and Repair Local Optima in Multilinear Graph QUBO Optimization

Yang Huang

Transactions on Machine Learning Research (TMLR) · Under Review

We propose a learned destroy-and-repair framework for improving local optima in multilinear graph QUBO optimization. A recurrent repair network optimizes the relaxed QUBO energy, while a destroy policy learns a state-dependent destruction rate from post-repair discrete-score improvements.

Selected Projects

OT-Guided Stochastic Control for Graph Optimization

  • Unified MIS, MaxCut, and MaxClique as QUBO objectives with exact multilinear relaxations.
  • Derived controlled diffusion dynamics from an entropic OT / Schrödinger bridge perspective.
  • Used Laplace approximation and GNN amortization to obtain scalable control policies.
  • Evaluated on RB, ER, and BA graph distributions with competitive solution quality.

Learned Destroy and Repair for Multilinear QUBO

  • Designed a recurrent repair network trained by the original multilinear QUBO objective.
  • Trained a destroy policy with graph-level improvement rewards using policy gradients.
  • Used rate-matched random masks to reduce unreliable node-level credit assignment.
  • Improved repair-only inference on MIS and MaxCut under matched repair-call budgets.

Relational Attention Learned Optimizer for ATSP

  • Formulated asymmetric TSP as a city-position assignment QUBO over permutation matrices.
  • Designed relational attention over forward, backward, same-city, and same-position relations.
  • Combined relaxed ATSP objectives, row/column penalties, binary regularization, and Sinkhorn projection.
  • Exploring two-stage untied unrolled optimizers for construction and refinement.
